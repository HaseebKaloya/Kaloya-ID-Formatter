Pattern Guide & Reference

A pattern is a sequence of numbers separated by hyphens. Each number defines how many characters go into that segment. The formatter strips any existing hyphens from your input, then rebuilds it according to the pattern — inserting hyphens at exactly the right positions. Use rest as the final segment to capture all remaining characters regardless of length.

6-rest 6 characters, then everything else ABCDEF-GHIJKLMNO
If an input is shorter than the pattern expects, the formatter simply formats what it has and stops cleanly — no errors, no leftover hyphens. Inputs that already contain hyphens are automatically stripped and reformatted from scratch.
